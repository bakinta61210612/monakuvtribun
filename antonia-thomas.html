Early life and Bio

Thomas was born on 3rd November of 1986 in the capital city of England, London. Her full name is Antonia Laura Thomas, and her father is a famous English classical singer, David Thomas. Her nationality is English, and she got her African looks from her Jamaican mother. She is the youngest among her sister Emma Jay Thomas and Chloe Lucy Thomas. Her eldest sister is also an actress, and the other sister is a journalist.

Thomas completed her education in 2009 from the Bristol Old Vic Theatre School in Bristol with a bachelor’s degree in arts. Then, she made her theatre debut at National Youth Theatre in London.

Movies and Tv shows

Antonia Thomas made her film debut in 2012. She played a small role in the film Eight Minutes Idle as Adrienne. The same year, she also appeared in Mat Whitecross’s British comedy film, Spike Island, portraying Lisa’s character. Following year, she appeared in Dexter Fletcher’s romantic musical film, Sunshine on Leith, performing Yvonne’s name, and in the comedy-drama film called Hello Carter as Mischa.

In 2014, Thomas made her appearance in Elaine Constantine’s historical film Northern Soul portraying Angela’s character, and she also appeared in Billy O’Brien’s science fiction horror film The Hybrid as Steinmann. In 2015, she played the role of Naomi Rosenbaum in an action spy film, Survivor.

After that, Thomas has appeared in many other films like FirstBorn, rearview, and Freedoms Name Is Mighty Sweet.

Antonia Thomas started off her television career from the hit British science fiction comedy television series called Misfits. She played the leading role as Alisha Daniels, and the show was premiered in 2009 and ended in 2011. She also made her appearance in the television drama called Stanley Park as Sadie and in the tv series drama The Deep as Maddy appearing in 3 episodes in 2010.

In 2012, Thomas appeared in the TV miniseries, Homefront, as Tasha Raveley. In 2014, she landed another central role in the British sitcom Lovesick portraying the character of Evelyn “Evie” Douglas. The series was successful and ended in 2018. She also worked as a narrator for the British children television series Teletubbies from 2015 until 2018.

In 2017, Antonia Thomas landed another significant role in the American medical drama televisions series called The Good Doctor, where she portrayed the character of Dr. Claire Brown. The series received high success, and as of 2020, it is still running.

Thomas has played small roles in many television series like Fleming and Transporter: The Series in 2014, The Musketeers and The Ark in 2015, The nightmare Worlds of H.G Well in 2016, and Snatches: Moments from Women’s Lives in 2018.
