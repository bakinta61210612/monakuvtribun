Debra Jeter: Is Debra Jeter Still Alive

 Posted on August 20, 2024  |  3 minutes  |  480 words  |  Martina Birk

In early June 2009, Debra Jeter became a headline sensation when she made an infamous 911 call, confessing to the killing of her two children.

Surprisingly, 13-year-old Kiersten, one of her daughters, was not dead but fighting for survival.

Despite being a nurse, Debra declined to provide any assistance and instead leisurely walked around the house while awaiting emergency services.

Debra Jeter Biography

Debra Janelle Jeter, an American mother, gained notoriety for her horrifying attempt to kill her two children, Kiersten and Kelsey Jeter.

Unfortunately, this tragic incident on June 5, 2009, resulted in the death of 12-year-old Kelsey and left 13-year-old Kiersten struggling for her life.

Born on November 20, 1962, in Randolph County, Georgia, Debra Jeter is nearing her 60th birthday. Her parents were the late Billy Jeter and Palona Jeter.

Debra worked as a Program Specialist in Academic Affairs for West Georgia Technical College.

Relationship with Lee Jeter

The details of when Debra and Lester, also known as Lee, started their relationship are unknown.

However, what we do know is that their love story didn’t have a happy ending.

Lee initiated separation proceedings in May 2009. A neighbor living down the street from the Jeters disclosed that their home often had disturbances, leading to police visits.

While it remains unclear why Lee filed for separation, what is certain is that the news deeply affected Debra.

Consequently, on May 31, 2009, she attempted suicide in front of her two children.

This distressing incident prompted Lee to request a restraining order against Debra, fearing for their children’s safety.

Surprisingly, the court granted Debra unsupervised visitation just three weeks after the restraining order was lifted.

Unfortunately, this decision would later expose the two vulnerable young girls to danger.

Debra Jeter and the chilling 911 call

Jeter committed the crime and dialed 911, confessing to the operator that she had killed her children.

She mentioned that one child was dead, but the other needed emergency assistance.

During the call, Jeter comforted the injured Kiersten and begged the operator not to have the police shoot her, emphasizing that she didn’t possess a gun.

Deputies arrived at the scene, discovering Debra with her hands raised and the murder weapon placed on the roof of a nearby vehicle.

They promptly arrested her and placed her on suicide watch at Hill County Law Enforcement Center.

Is Debra Jeter still alive?

While there are speculations about her taking her life while incarcerated in a Texas Department of Criminal Justice prison unit in Gatesville, there is no concrete evidence of her death.

On the other hand, her ex-husband Lee found love again and remarried.
