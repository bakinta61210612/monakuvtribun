Missing Muskogee Woman Amanda Harms Found Dead

 Posted on August 30, 2024  |  2 minutes  |  218 words  |  Billy Koelling

In a sorrowful update from the Muskogee Police Department, Amanda “Mandi” Harms, a 46-year-old woman who had been missing since Saturday, January 13, 2024, was found dead on Thursday, January 18, 2024.

Amanda Harms, known to her students as Ms. Harms, was a beloved teacher in the Muskogee community. She was last seen leaving her residence on foot from the 4400 block of Denison Street. Her disappearance prompted an extensive search and appeal to the public for any information regarding her whereabouts.

Tragically, her body was discovered in a yard in the 600 block of West Martin. The cause of her death is currently under investigation. As news of her death spread through the community, tributes started pouring in for the well-respected educator.

The loss of Amanda Harms has sent shockwaves through the community, with many expressing their grief and extending their condolences to her family. The Muskogee Police Department has asked for respect and privacy for the Harms family during this difficult time.

As we remember Amanda Harms, we acknowledge her dedication to education and her impact on the lives of her students. Our deepest sympathies go out to her family, friends, and the entire Muskogee community.
