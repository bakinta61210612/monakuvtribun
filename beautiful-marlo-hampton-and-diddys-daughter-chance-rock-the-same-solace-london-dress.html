Beautiful: Marlo Hampton and Diddys Daughter Chance Rock the Same Solace London Dress

 Posted on August 27, 2024  |  2 minutes  |  411 words  |  Kelle Repass

It seems Sean “Diddy” Combs’ 14-year-old daughter Chance Combs has the exact same taste as her elder Marlo Hampton.

Both ladies were spotted poised in a pink, orange ombre mini dress by Solace London. The asymmetrical ruffle-shoulder ensemble retails at $570 and was crafted from a “textured crepe,” according to the British brand’s website.

Chance was seen wearing the Solace dress on Jan. 25 at the Pre-Grammy Gala to honor her father. She complemented the look with orange and pink strapped sandal heels, a pink clutch and dangle diamond earrings. The teenager kept her makeup basic and wore her hair in a topknot bun.

The “Real Housewives of Atlanta” star sported the dress to an after-show with Bravo Daily Dish last week. She rocked burgundy stiletto pumps with the ensemble and jazzed up her guise with gold accessories. Hampton wore her hair in cornrows and sported a high-glam makeup look, including a brown lippie. She added in her caption, “The after show …. #rhoa”

Fans gushed over both Chance and Hampton in their stunning ensembles.

Hampton isn’t just known for her expensive taste in fashion, she’s also a staple in the hair industry.

Just a few weeks ago, the HER Hair collection founder explained to her fans why she arguably is obsessed with wigs.

“I love wearing wigs as protective styles so my natural hair can rest, hydrate and grow,” she said via Instagram. “I change my hair often to coordinate with my outfits (as any real fashion girl does) an outfit may call for a certain hair color or cut to pull off the look. Over processing and over styling natural hair causes so much damage.”

Hampton says she created her hair line to help women experiencing hair damage.

“I created @her_hair_collection for women like me!! Women who are not ashamed to throw on a luxurious impeccably styled wig and be fabulous, women suffering from health issues and hair loss or women just trying to repair their natural from years or wear and tear.”
